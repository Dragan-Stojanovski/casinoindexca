What is a low wagering bonus?

A low wagering bonus is one with a small, realistic playthrough requirement — usually between 1× and 20× — that you can actually clear, unlike the 40× or 45× attached to typical welcome offers. The wagering requirement is the number of times you must bet a bonus before its winnings become withdrawable, and it's the single biggest factor in whether a bonus is worth anything. Lower wagering means less of the house edge grinding away on forced bets, which means far more of the bonus survives as real, withdrawable value. As the calculator above shows, dropping from 45× to 5× can be the difference between keeping a few percent of a bonus and keeping almost all of it.

Why low wagering beats a big headline

Players fixate on the size of a bonus and ignore the number that actually determines its worth. Consider a $100 reward. At 5× wagering, you must bet $500 to clear it — trivial, and you keep roughly $83 of real value. At 45×, you must bet $4,500, and the house-edge cost of doing so wipes out most of the bonus. Same $100 headline, wildly different outcomes. This is why we rank low-wagering offers so highly and spotlight the wagering figure directly on each casino's card: it's the honest measure of value, and it's usually buried in the terms rather than the banner.

How to spot genuinely low wagering

  1. Find the multiplier first — before the headline amount. Anything at or below 20× is worth a closer look.
  2. Check what it applies to — "bonus only" is far better than "deposit + bonus".
  3. Watch for a win cap — a low cap can quietly limit even a low-wagering reward.
  4. Favour cashback and cash rewards — they carry the lowest wagering.
  5. Run the numbers — the calculator above turns any offer into a real-value figure in seconds.
