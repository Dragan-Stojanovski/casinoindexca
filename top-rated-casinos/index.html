The four pillars in depth

The simulator above shows how the score is built; here's what each pillar actually measures and why it carries the weight it does.

Licensing & Trust — 35%

This is the largest single weight because it's the foundation everything else rests on. We check who regulates the casino, how strict that regulator is, and whether the licence can be independently verified — not just claimed. A Malta Gaming Authority licence, the strictest mainstream regulator, sits at the top; long-established regulators like Kahnawake and modern Curaçao GCB licences follow. Brand track record feeds in here too: a decade of clean operation is worth more than any marketing. Where a licence can't be verified, we say so plainly and the score is capped — an unverifiable licence means no meaningful recourse if a payout is withheld.

Payout Reliability — 30%

The second-largest weight, because a casino that won't pay is worthless no matter how good it looks. We deposit our own money, play, and request real withdrawals across Interac, cards and crypto, timing each one end to end. We look at pending-review windows, processing speed, withdrawal limits and whether the KYC process is reasonable or a stalling tactic. A casino can lose serious points here even with a great licence — reliability is proven, not promised.

Terms Integrity — 20%

Here we read the fine print line by line so you don't have to. Wagering requirements, maximum-bet rules during bonuses, win caps on "no wagering" rewards, game weighting and withdrawal restrictions all get scrutinised. Predatory terms — a 45× rollover, a hidden cap that guts a big win — cost an operator points even if it pays quickly, because unfair terms are how a "generous" bonus quietly becomes worthless.

Dispute History — 15%

The smallest but still meaningful weight. We look at complaint volume relative to the casino's size, the nature of those complaints, and — most importantly — how the operator resolves them. Every casino gets the occasional dispute; what separates a trustworthy one is fast, fair resolution rather than stonewalling. A pattern of unresolved payout complaints is one of the strongest negative signals there is.
