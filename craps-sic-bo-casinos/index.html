Craps — complex table, simple core bets

Craps has a reputation for being intimidating, and the busy table layout doesn't help — but the truth is that its core bets are simple and among the best value in the casino. The Pass Line and Don't Pass bets carry a house edge of just ~1.4%, and backing them with free odds bets lowers the overall edge even further. Ignore the flashy proposition and hardways bets in the middle of the table (they carry a high edge) and craps becomes one of the smartest, most exciting games you can play. Sic Bo — the easiest dice game

If craps feels like too much, Sic Bo is the perfect entry point. This ancient Chinese three-dice game has no shooter, no rolling sequence and no complex flow — you simply bet on the outcome of three dice each round. The Big (11–17) and Small (4–10) bets carry a low ~2.8% edge and are as easy as red/black in roulette. Specific triples pay huge (up to 180:1) but carry a high edge, so keep those small. What is craps?

Craps is a dice game where you bet on the outcome of two dice. The core Pass Line and Don't Pass bets carry a low house edge (~1.4%), which drops further with odds bets. It looks complex but the basic bets are simple, making it one of the better-value dice games.

What is Sic Bo?

Sic Bo is an ancient Chinese dice game where you bet on the outcome of three dice. Small (4–10) and Big (11–17) bets carry a low ~2.8% house edge, while specific triples pay big but carry a high edge. It's simpler than craps and popular in live casinos.
