The scoring model

The Safety Index, explained in full.

Every casino we list is scored out of 10 against four fixed, weighted pillars. The model is published in full — so you can check our work, hold us to it, and be certain that no operator can quietly buy its way up the list.

The four pillars

The 35 / 30 / 20 / 15 weighting is fixed and applied identically to every operator. A weakness in any single pillar caps the overall score — a great licence cannot compensate for unreliable payouts.

Why we weight, rather than average

The Safety Index is deliberately not a flat average of four numbers, because the four factors are not equally important. A casino could have the biggest game library and the slickest app in Canada, but if its licence is unverifiable and its payouts stall, it is not safe — and the weighting ensures those failings dominate the score rather than being averaged away by cosmetic strengths. Licensing and payouts together make up 65% of the score for exactly this reason: they are what stand between you and your money.

Pillar 1 — Licensing & Trust (35%)

This carries the largest weight because it is the foundation everything else rests on. For every operator we identify the regulator, confirm the licence number against that regulator's own public register, and assess how much protection the licence actually affords. A Malta Gaming Authority licence — with strict fund-segregation, audit and dispute-resolution requirements — sits at the top; long-established regulators such as the Kahnawake Gaming Commission and the reformed Curaçao Gaming Control Board follow; and Ontario's iGaming Ontario / AGCO framework provides strong domestic recourse for Ontario players. We also weigh corporate transparency (is a real operating company named?), enforcement history, and third-party game-fairness certification. Where a licence cannot be independently verified, we say so plainly and the score is capped — an unverifiable licence means no meaningful recourse if a payout is withheld.

Pillar 2 — Payout Reliability (30%)

The second-largest weight, because a casino that will not pay is worthless no matter how good it looks. We do not accept the word "instant" from a marketing page. We register a real account as a Canadian player, deposit our own money, play a normal session, and request genuine withdrawals across Interac, cards and crypto, timestamping two separate moments: when the request enters "pending", and when the money actually arrives. The gap between those, repeated across methods, produces the pending-review and processing figures on our pages. We deliberately test the first withdrawal from a fresh account, because that is when identity verification (KYC) is triggered and where most player complaints originate. We also record withdrawal limits, since a fast casino with a low weekly cap can still take weeks to release a big win.

Pillar 3 — Terms Integrity (20%)

Here we read the fine print so you don't have to. We scrutinise the wagering requirement and whether it applies to the bonus only or deposit-plus-bonus; the maximum-bet-while-wagering rule that can void a bonus if breached; the maximum cashout that can cap winnings from an offer; game weighting that decides which games count toward playthrough; and restrictive clauses around dormancy, inactivity fees and account closure. Predatory terms — a 45× rollover, a hidden win cap that guts a big win — cost an operator points even when it pays quickly, because unfair terms are how a "generous" bonus quietly becomes worthless.

Pillar 4 — Dispute Record (15%)

The smallest but still meaningful weight. Every casino attracts the occasional complaint; what separates a trustworthy operator is fast, fair resolution rather than stonewalling. We look at complaint volume relative to the casino's size, the nature of those complaints (payment delays and confiscated winnings weigh heaviest), and how the operator responds. A pattern of unresolved payout complaints is one of the strongest negative signals there is, and can move a score or trigger a flag on its own.

How we collect and verify the data

Scores are built from primary testing wherever possible, not operator marketing. Payout times come from live cashouts our team performs and logs. Licensing data is checked against the issuing regulator's public register on the date of audit. Bonus and account terms are read from the operator's own published documents, not summaries. Where we rely on third-party data — for example, aggregated complaint records — we weigh it against what we can verify ourselves and never treat a single unverified source as decisive.

Who does the testing

Reviews are produced by our editorial team and carry author bylines, with a second editor fact-checking each audit before publication. You can meet the people behind the scores, and read their areas of expertise, on our about page. Our approach to accuracy, sourcing and independence is set out in our editorial principles.

Refresh cadence

The index is reviewed on a rolling basis and market metrics refresh daily. Payout performance drifts — a brand can tighten or loosen its review process, switch payment providers, or change limits without warning — so any material change (a licence lapse, a payout slowdown, a spike in unresolved disputes) can move a score or trigger a flag at any time, not only on a fixed schedule.

When an operator is flagged or excluded

A flag means we have found something that puts player money or fairness at material risk: an unverifiable licence, systematic withdrawal delays, predatory terms, or a pattern of unresolved disputes. Flagged operators are excluded from our recommendations regardless of any commercial relationship. We would rather list fewer casinos we trust than pad the site with operators we don't.

Independence & conflict-of-interest policy

CasinoIndexCA earns affiliate commissions from some operators, which keeps the site free to use. Those commercial relationships are logged separately from scoring and carry zero weight in the Safety Index. A casino we are paid by that fails an audit is flagged, not promoted. We publish this model openly precisely so you can hold us to it — because a ranking you cannot scrutinise is one you should not trust, ours included.

Ontario adjustments

For Ontario visitors, the same underlying scores apply, but presentation follows AGCO advertising standards: bonus figures, free-spin credits and dollar inducements are not shown, and licensing, registration numbers, payment options and verified payout times are shown instead. Use the region switch in the header to move between Ontario and the rest of Canada.

What the Safety Index is not

Our score measures operator safety, fairness and reliability — it is not a prediction that you will win, and it is not financial or legal advice. All gambling carries a house edge and real financial risk. Everyone we list is strictly 19+ (or the legal age in your province). If gambling stops being fun, please see our responsible gambling resources.
