Why live baccarat appeals to Canadians

Baccarat is beloved for two reasons: it's dead simple and it carries a low house edge. There are no in-hand decisions — you just bet on the Banker, the Player or a Tie, and the fixed rules play the hand out. Backed correctly, baccarat is one of the best-value games on the floor. Live baccarat adds a real dealer and HD streaming to that elegant simplicity, which is why it's a favourite of both casual players and high rollers. Every casino here runs a full Evolution baccarat suite, including the popular Speed and Squeeze formats.

The one rule that matters: back the Banker

Baccarat's only real strategy is bet selection, and it's simple. The Banker bet has the lowest house edge at about 1.06%, even after the standard 5% commission on Banker wins. The Player bet is close at ~1.24%. The Tie bet looks tempting with its big payout but carries a punishing ~14% edge — avoid it. Consistently backing the Banker is the entire strategy, and it keeps baccarat among the best-value games you can play.

What is live baccarat?

Live baccarat streams a real dealer dealing cards in HD while you bet on the Player, Banker or Tie. It's simple — no decisions beyond your bet — and carries a low house edge on the Banker bet (~1.06%). Evolution offers standard, Speed and Squeeze versions.

What is the best bet in baccarat?

The Banker bet has the lowest house edge at about 1.06% (even after the standard 5% commission on wins). The Player bet is close at ~1.24%. The Tie bet has a very high edge (~14%) and should be avoided. Consistently backing the Banker is the standard strategy.

What is Squeeze baccarat?

Squeeze baccarat adds suspense by having the dealer slowly reveal (squeeze) the cards on camera, building tension before the result. The odds are identical to standard baccarat; only the presentation differs.

What is Speed baccarat?

Speed baccarat runs faster rounds — typically around 27 seconds versus 48 — for high-volume players who want more hands per hour. The rules and odds are the same as standard baccarat.

Is baccarat a game of skill?

No, baccarat is a game of chance with no in-hand decisions beyond your bet. The only 'strategy' is bet selection — consistently backing the Banker for the lowest house edge and avoiding the Tie bet.
