Why video poker is a hidden gem

Video poker is one of the best-value games in any casino, and one of the most overlooked. It's a solo game based on five-card draw poker: you're dealt five cards, choose which to hold, draw replacements for the rest, and get paid on your final hand's rank per the paytable. What makes it special is transparency and skill — the paytable is right there before you commit a cent, and your hold decisions directly affect the outcome. With the right variant and optimal play, top games return over 99%, far better than most slots. It rewards a little study, which is exactly why savvy players love it.

Read the paytable — it is the RTP

In video poker, the paytable literally sets your return. The classic benchmark is 9/6 Jacks or Better: "9/6" means 9 coins for a Full House and 6 for a Flush (per coin bet), which returns about 99.54% with optimal play. Lower-paying versions (8/5, 7/5) quietly cut the RTP. Full-pay Deuces Wild can theoretically exceed 100%. Before you play any video poker game, glance at the Full House and Flush payouts — that one check tells you whether you're on a good machine.

What is video poker?

Video poker is a solo card game based on five-card draw poker. You're dealt five cards, choose which to hold, and draw replacements; you're paid on the final hand's rank per the paytable. With optimal play, top variants like 9/6 Jacks or Better return over 99%.

Which video poker game has the best RTP?

Full-pay (9/6) Jacks or Better returns about 99.54% with optimal play, and full-pay Deuces Wild can exceed 100% in theory. The paytable is everything — always check the payouts for a Full House and Flush before playing, as they determine the RTP.

Is video poker skill-based?

Yes. Unlike slots, your hold decisions directly affect the outcome, and there's a mathematically correct play for every hand. Learning optimal strategy — often via a strategy chart — is what unlocks video poker's high 99%+ returns.

What does 9/6 Jacks or Better mean?

9/6 refers to the paytable: 9 coins for a Full House and 6 for a Flush (per coin bet). This 'full-pay' version returns ~99.54% with optimal play. Lower-paying versions (8/5, 7/5) reduce the RTP, so always check the paytable first.

Should I bet max coins in video poker?

Usually yes. Betting the maximum (5 coins) unlocks the enhanced Royal Flush payout, which significantly boosts the RTP. Adjust the coin value down if needed to keep your bet affordable, but bet 5 coins to get the full return.
