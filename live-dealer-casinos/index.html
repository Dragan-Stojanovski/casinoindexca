Evolution's game shows explained

Game shows are the reason live casino exploded in popularity, and Evolution dominates the category. They blend the spectacle of a TV format with real-money betting, hosted by a presenter rather than a dealer. If you're new to them, here's what the headline titles actually offer.

Crazy Time is the flagship — a giant money wheel with four bonus rounds (Cash Hunt, Pachinko, Coin Flip and the Crazy Time wheel itself) where multipliers can climb into the tens of thousands. Live blackjack: the strategy player's floor

If value is your priority, live blackjack is the single best game in any live casino, with a house edge around 0.5% when you play basic strategy correctly. Live blackjack adds a social, real-dealer layer to that math, and the best floors offer several ways to get a seat even when tables are full. Bet Behind lets you wager on another player's hand when all seats are taken, while Infinite Blackjack seats unlimited players on a single dealer's hand. Watch the rules at each table: 3:2 blackjack payouts are far better than 6:5, and side bets like Perfect Pairs and 21+3 are fun but carry much higher house edges, so keep them small. What you need for a smooth live stream

Live dealer is the most bandwidth-hungry thing you'll do at an online casino, because you're receiving a real-time HD video feed. A stable connection matters more than a fast one: a steady 5–10 Mbps handles HD comfortably, but an unstable link that drops in and out will buffer at the worst moments. Play on Wi-Fi or a strong mobile signal rather than a patchy connection, close bandwidth-heavy apps in the background, and if you're on mobile, be mindful of data usage since a long live session streams a lot of video. Most studios let you lower the stream quality if your connection struggles — a smoother lower-resolution feed beats a stuttering HD one every time.

What is Crazy Time and how does it work?

Crazy Time is Evolution's flagship live game show — a giant money wheel with four bonus rounds (Cash Hunt, Pachinko, Coin Flip and the Crazy Time wheel) where multipliers can reach the tens of thousands. It's built for entertainment and big-hit potential rather than the tight odds of blackjack or baccarat.

What's the difference between Bet Behind and Infinite Blackjack?

Bet Behind lets you wager on a seated player's hand when all seats are taken, while Infinite Blackjack seats unlimited players on a single dealer's hand. Both let you play live blackjack without waiting for a free seat at busy tables.

Is 3:2 or 6:5 blackjack better?

3:2 is significantly better for the player. It pays 1.5× your bet on a blackjack versus only 1.2× at 6:5, which meaningfully raises the house edge. Always choose 3:2 tables and keep side bets like Perfect Pairs and 21+3 small, as they carry higher edges.
