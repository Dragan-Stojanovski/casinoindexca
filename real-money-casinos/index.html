Interac: the Canadian real-money standard

No payment method is more woven into Canadian online gambling than Interac. Because it links directly to the account you already bank with, there's no third-party wallet to fund and no card details to share with the casino — the transfer happens through the domestic banking network you already trust. Deposits are effectively instant, and withdrawals typically land in 1–3 business days. Two forms exist: Interac e-Transfer, the familiar email-based transfer, and Interac Online, a direct bank-to-merchant payment. Most casinos support one or both, and many set their lowest deposit minimum on Interac, often just $10. The one limitation is the banking calendar: Interac withdrawals don't process on weekends or holidays, so if weekend payouts matter to you, pair Interac deposits with a faster withdrawal method or use the matcher above.

Do you pay tax on casino winnings in Canada?

For the large majority of Canadian players, casino winnings are not taxable. The Canada Revenue Agency treats gambling as a windfall rather than income for recreational players, so a lucky slot win or a good night at blackjack is generally yours to keep in full. The nuance is for professional gamblers who play as a business — a rare category with a high legal bar — whose winnings can be treated as taxable income. There's also a wrinkle worth knowing: winnings from US-based gambling can be subject to US withholding tax even for Canadians, though that rarely applies to online play at the offshore and Ontario-licensed casinos covered here. This is general information rather than tax advice; if your situation is unusual or you play at a professional level, consult a qualified accountant. For nearly everyone playing recreationally, though, what you win is what you keep.
