What are progressive jackpot slots?

A progressive jackpot is a prize that grows with every bet placed across a whole network of casinos until one lucky player triggers it and wins the entire pool. Because thousands of players feed the same jackpot simultaneously, the prize can climb into the millions of dollars — the kind of win that genuinely changes a life. The two most famous networks, both from Microgaming, are Mega Moolah (the record-breaking African-safari classic) and WowPot. The trade-off is simple and worth understanding: a slice of every bet is diverted to fund the growing jackpot, which lowers the base return-to-player. You're trading day-to-day odds for a shot at the dream.

Jackpots vs high-RTP slots — the trade-off

It's worth being clear-eyed. Progressive jackpot slots carry a lower base RTP precisely because part of every bet feeds the prize pool. That makes them poorer value on ordinary spins than a 96.5% non-jackpot slot. The right way to think about them is as two different activities: high-RTP slots for stretching your bankroll, and progressive jackpots for the occasional shot at a life-changing sum. Enjoy the dream, but keep it in the entertainment column of your budget.
