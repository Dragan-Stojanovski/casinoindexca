Software roulette, explained

Online (RNG) roulette uses a certified random number generator to decide where the ball lands, rather than a physical wheel. That makes it fast, available at any stake, and playable at your own pace — ideal for learning the bets or fitting in a quick session. The rules and odds are identical to a real wheel; only the presentation differs. When you want a real croupier and the drama of a spinning wheel, our live roulette guide covers that.

Choose the right wheel — it halves the edge

As with live roulette, the wheel you choose decides your odds. A European (single-zero) wheel has a house edge of about 2.7%; an American (double-zero) wheel is nearly double at 5.26%. French roulette with La Partage can be even better on even-money bets. Always pick single-zero — it's free odds, simply by selecting the right table.

Inside vs outside bets

Roulette bets split into two families. Inside bets — straight-up numbers, splits, corners — pay big (up to 35:1) but win rarely, giving high variance. Outside bets — red/black, odd/even, dozens, columns — pay less but win far more often, for a steadier ride. On a single-zero wheel the house edge is the same across bet types; the choice is purely about how much variance you want. Beginners often start with outside bets for longer, gentler sessions.

A word on betting systems

No betting system — Martingale, Fibonacci or any other — can overcome the house edge in the long run. They can produce short-term wins, but they risk large losses and quickly hit table limits. Roulette is a game of chance; the only real "strategy" is choosing a single-zero wheel, deciding your budget in advance, and treating it as entertainment. Anyone promising a guaranteed roulette win is selling a myth.
