How to play live blackjack the right way

  1. Use basic strategy — the mathematically correct move for every hand keeps the edge near 0.5%. Keep a chart handy.
  2. Choose 3:2 tables — they pay far better than 6:5 on a blackjack.
  3. Keep side bets small — Perfect Pairs and 21+3 are fun but carry a high house edge.
  4. Play on a stable connection — live streams need steady bandwidth; use Wi-Fi.
  5. Note bonus weighting — live blackjack often counts 0% toward wagering; see no wagering casinos.

Live vs RNG blackjack

Both use the same rules and basic strategy, and both offer excellent odds. Live blackjack adds a real dealer, atmosphere and social interaction, streamed in real time. RNG (software) blackjack is faster, available at any stake, and lets you play at your own pace — great for drilling strategy. If you want the experience, go live; if you want speed and solitude, our RNG blackjack guide has you covered.

What is live blackjack?

Live blackjack streams a real human dealer from a studio in HD while you bet from your device. It combines the low ~0.5% house edge of blackjack (with basic strategy) with the atmosphere of a real table. Evolution is the leading provider.

What is the house edge on live blackjack?

Around 0.5% when you play basic strategy correctly on a 3:2 table with good rules — one of the lowest edges in any casino. Deviating from strategy, taking side bets, or playing 6:5 tables raises the edge.

What are Lightning and Infinite Blackjack?

Lightning Blackjack adds random multipliers to winning hands for a small fee per round, while Infinite Blackjack seats unlimited players on one dealer's hand. Both are Evolution formats that let you play even when standard tables are full.

Is 3:2 or 6:5 blackjack better?

3:2 is much better for the player — it pays 1.5x your bet on a blackjack versus only 1.2x at 6:5, which meaningfully raises the house edge. Always choose 3:2 tables and keep side bets small.

Can I use basic strategy on live blackjack?

Yes. Basic strategy — the mathematically correct action for every hand — applies exactly the same in live blackjack as RNG blackjack, and using it keeps the house edge near 0.5%. Keep a strategy chart handy while you play.
