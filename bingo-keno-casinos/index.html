Bingo and keno — the casual classics

If you want gambling at its most relaxed, bingo and keno are it. Both are lottery-style number games that require no strategy and no fast decisions — you pick or receive numbers, a draw takes place, and you're paid on the matches. The appeal is simplicity and the dream of a big payout from a small stake. They're the perfect games for a low-key session or playing alongside a chat, and they're widely available at Canadian-facing casinos. Just go in knowing they carry a higher house edge than table games — the trade-off for that easy, lottery-style thrill.

How keno works

Keno is the online casino's version of a lottery. You choose a set of numbers — usually up to 10 or 20 — from a pool of 80, then a draw reveals 20 numbers. Your payout depends on how many of your picks match, per the game's paytable. Picking fewer numbers improves your hit rate but caps the top prize; picking more chases bigger payouts with longer odds. There's no skill beyond that choice, so pick a paytable and count you enjoy, and set a budget. Keno is fast, casual and available at SoftSwiss casinos like Bitkingz, Crocoslots and Oshi.

How online bingo works

Online bingo takes the beloved hall game and automates it. You buy tickets, numbers are drawn automatically, and auto-daub marks your matches so you never miss one. You win by completing a pattern or a full house, often with community jackpots and chat rooms adding a social layer. It's the most relaxed game in any casino — no decisions, just the fun of watching your numbers come up. Big brands including Jackpot City offer bingo-style and keno games.
